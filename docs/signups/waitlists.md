# Waitlists

When a session fills up, DailyPB automatically enables a **Waitlist** so additional players can sign up for a chance to join if a spot opens.

![What a waitlisted player sees when viewing a Session.](/img/notion-help/waitlists/image.png)

What a waitlisted player sees when viewing a Session.

### How It Works

Once a session reaches capacity:

- The **Join** button automatically changes to **Join Waitlist**.
- Players can then join the **Waitlist**.
- They can view their **Waitlist position** anytime on the **Attendees screen**

---

### 💡 Auto-Bumping Feature

Whenever a player is removed from a session — either by themselves or by the host — the first person on the Waitlist is **automatically promoted** into the open spot.

**Notifications sent automatically:**

- The **removed player** and **host** get an email confirming the removal.
- The **promoted player** receives both an **email and text message** letting them know they’ve been bumped up to “Attending.”
- If payment is required, the notification will include a reminder to complete payment promptly to keep their spot.
- The **host** also gets an email confirming who was bumped from the Waitlist and who is being promoted.
- Note that the Auto-bumping feature only works if there is a spot open.

# How to Swap an Attending Player With Someone Who Isn’t Signed Up

Use this when you need to replace a player who is currently attending with a new player who has not yet signed up.

**Steps**

1. Open the Session and go to the **Attending** list.
2. Find the player you want to remove and click “**Move to Waitlist**.” (This opens up a spot without auto-bumping or notifications).
3. Click “**Add Player**” from the main Session screen
4. Since there is an open spot in the Session (from step 2) it will sign them up to the main roster and bypass the Waitlist.
5. You can now remove the player you moved to the Waitlist from step 2. There will be an email notifying the player they are off the waitlist.

This keeps your attendee list accurate and ensures the new player is properly added to the Session.

# How to Swap an Attending Player with a Waitlisted Player

Use this when you want to replace a current attendee with someone who is already on the waitlist.

**Steps**

1. Open the Session and go to the **Attending** list.
2. Find the player you want to remove and click “Move to Waitlist.” 
3. Go to the **Waitlist** section.
4. Select the waitlisted player you want to bump up.
5. Click **Bump to Attending**.

This instantly frees a spot and promotes the chosen waitlisted player into the Session.

# 🔄 Moving a Player to or from the Waitlist Manually

Hosts can manually move players from **Attending** to **Waitlist** once a session is full (or overbooked)

This is helpful if:

- You want to swap an attending player with someone on the Waitlist.
- You need manual control over who participates in a specific session.

When a host (or player) manually moves someone 👇🏻 to the Waitlist:

- The **Auto-Bumping feature will not trigger**.
- No notifications will be sent
- The move is handled manually to give hosts flexibility in managing rosters.
- There should now be an open spot on the roster in which you can (quickly) sign someone up and thereby bypass the Waitlist

When a host bumps someone up ☝🏻 from the Waitlist to Attending manually 

- The player will receive an email informing them that a spot opened up and they are next up on the waitlist. It will ask them to confirm by paying (if needed) or cancel if they cannot attend.
- If the Session uses [Auto Payments](/docs/payments/automatic-payments) and there is a payment due, the player will also receive a text message
- If the host is using [Manual Payments](/docs/payments/manual-payments), then a DailyPB fee will be accrued.  (This is reversed if the player cancels).

# 🕰️Waitlist Reminder Emails (24 Hour Notice)

When [**Reminders**](/docs/sessions/2-24-hr-reminders) (this is on by default) are enabled for a Session, everyone on the Waitlist automatically receives an email **24 hours before the Session starts**. This message reminds them that they’re still on the waitlist and includes a **one-click link to remove themselves** if they know they can’t attend or have made other plans. This keeps your waitlist accurate and active.

If a spot opens up, the system checks with the first person on the waitlist and **waits for them to cancel** before moving to the next player. Because Waitlisters were already prompted to opt-out when they can’t make it, confirmations happen faster and the Session fills more smoothly—cutting down on last-minute no-shows and stalled waitlists.

### Summary

Waitlists ensure fairness and flexibility in popular sessions.

They:

- Let players hold a spot in line.
- Automatically fill openings.
- Keep everyone notified in real time.
