One of the most common questions I hear from new pickleball hosts is: *"What format should I use for my tournament?"* The answer depends on your group size, available court time, and how competitive your players are. Here's a breakdown of the most popular formats.

<!-- truncate -->

## Round Robin

**Best for:** Social events, smaller groups (8–20 players), league play

In a round robin, every player or team plays every other player or team in their division. Nobody gets eliminated — you just accumulate wins and losses, and standings are determined at the end.

**Pros:**
- Maximum court time for everyone
- One bad match doesn't ruin the day
- Great for social/recreational events
- Easy to manage with DailyPB's Round Robin App

**Cons:**
- Takes longer as the group size grows
- Can feel anticlimactic at the end — no "big final match"
- Harder to run with more than 20 players per division

**Example schedule:** 12 players, 6 courts → 11 rounds, each round ~15 minutes → ~3 hours total

---

## Single Elimination

**Best for:** Large fields, limited time, competitive events

Every match is win-or-go-home. Lose once and you're done.

**Pros:**
- Fast — half the field is eliminated each round
- High stakes = high energy
- Easy bracket math

**Cons:**
- Half the players go home after one match
- One bad game (or a tough first-round draw) can knock out a strong player
- Not great for player experience at recreational events

**When to use it:** When you have 32+ players and only 4 hours of court time.

---

## Double Elimination

**Best for:** Competitive events where you want a true champion

Every player gets at least two matches. Lose once, you go to the consolation bracket. Lose twice, you're out.

**Pros:**
- Best player almost always wins
- Players get more matches
- The consolation bracket keeps engagement high

**Cons:**
- Complex scheduling — you need to track two brackets
- Runs longer than single elimination
- Harder to manage without software

This is the format I'd recommend for any DailyPB event with 16–32 players where competition matters.

---

## Ladder / King of the Court

**Best for:** Drop-in events, open play with a competitive twist

Players start at different positions on the "ladder." Win and you move up; lose and you move down (or rotate out). The top position at the end wins.

**Pros:**
- Fun and dynamic — positions are always changing
- Works great for drop-in style events
- Players can join mid-session

**Cons:**
- Less structured than bracket play
- Starting position can heavily influence final results

DailyPB's Ladder App handles the position tracking automatically, making this format much easier to run than the old whiteboard method.

## Which Should You Choose?

| Format | Group Size | Time Required | Best For |
|--------|-----------|---------------|----------|
| Round Robin | 8–20 | 2–4 hrs | Social, league play |
| Single Elimination | 16+ | 1–3 hrs | Large competitive events |
| Double Elimination | 8–32 | 3–5 hrs | Competitive, serious players |
| Ladder | Any | Flexible | Drop-in, open play |
