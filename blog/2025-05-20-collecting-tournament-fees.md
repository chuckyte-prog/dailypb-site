If you've ever run a pickleball tournament and collected fees at the door, you know the chaos: someone forgot their wallet, someone wants to pay in cash but you only have $20 bills, three people didn't show up and you're out $60 in reserved court time.

Collecting fees upfront fixes all of this. Here's how to do it right.

<!-- truncate -->

## Why Upfront Payments Matter

**No-shows drop dramatically.** When someone has already paid $20–$40 to play, they show up. When they haven't paid yet, it's easy to bail at the last minute.

**You know your real headcount.** Planning court assignments, bracket sizes, and snack quantities gets much easier when you know exactly who's committed.

**Less awkwardness day-of.** Handling money during an event is distracting. Upfront payments let you focus on running the tournament.

## How to Set It Up on DailyPB

DailyPB's Automatic Payment system connects to Stripe, so players can pay via credit card, Apple Pay, or Google Pay when they register — no app download required.

Here's the setup flow:

1. **Create your tournament session** and set a price per player
2. **Enable Automatic Payments** in the payment settings
3. **Set your refund deadline** — we recommend 48 hours before the event
4. Players sign up and pay at checkout — they get a confirmation email automatically
5. Funds flow to your Stripe account after the event

The whole setup takes about 5 minutes if you already have a Stripe account. If not, Stripe onboarding takes 10–15 minutes.

## What to Charge

Tournament fees vary widely based on location, amenities, and format. A few benchmarks:

- **Casual round robin (2–3 hours):** $10–$20 per player
- **Half-day competitive tournament:** $25–$40 per player
- **Full-day event with prizes:** $40–$75 per player

If you're providing balls, prizes, or refreshments, build those costs into the fee. Most players are happy to pay a fair price for a well-organized event.

## Handling Refunds

Set a clear refund policy and display it prominently before players pay. With DailyPB's automatic refund system, players who cancel before your refund deadline get their money back automatically — you don't have to process anything manually.

After the deadline, it's up to you whether to issue a discretionary refund. The system gives you a manual refund button for any transaction, any time.

## Alternatives to Automatic Payments

If you prefer to handle payments outside DailyPB (cash, Venmo, Zelle), the Manual Payment Tracking feature lets you mark players as paid or unpaid directly in the attendee list. Not as seamless, but it keeps your player list organized and gives you a record of who owes what.
