# Round Robin / Ladder App

This app enables hosts to set up Round-Robin brackets, record Game scores, and view results. Additionally, it allows the creation of new Rounds, where Players can be moved up or down the brackets in a format known as a Ladder.

<aside>
💡

A **pickleball round robin** is a format where players are divided into groups or brackets, and each player or team competes against every other player or team in their group. This ensures balanced matchups and allows participants to play multiple games against different opponents.

</aside>

# How to Create a Round Robin

1. In the Attendees Popup, under Session Apps, click on Round Robin & Ladders to load the Round Robin page.
2. Click on either Round Robin or Round Robin Ladder. You can also toggle back and forth between these two.
3. You will now see a few new areas with information on your screen. 
    1. Number of brackets selection (Round Robin) or Player per bracket selection (Round Robin Ladder). You can click on the numbered buttons to get as close as possible to how you want to arrange your Session. You will be able to fine-tune this later.
        
        ![image.png](Round%20Robin%20Ladder%20App/image.png)
        
    2. Create Games - when all brackets are set, click this button to create all the Games for your Round Robin or Round Robin Ladder
    3. Totals Dashboard
        
        ![image.png](Round%20Robin%20Ladder%20App/image%201.png)
        
        1. Players - total number of Players playing in the Round Robin or Ladder
        2. Courts - total number of courts needed for the Round Robin or Ladder
        3. Time - total time needed (in hours) for the Round Robin or Ladder. (This is based on 20 minutes per Game)
        4. Rounds - total rounds needed for the Round Robin or Ladder
    4. Brackets - this is where you can move around Players into Brackets or into the Not Playing bracket.
        1. To add a bracket, click on the “Add bracket” button below the Totals Dashboard.
        2. The top of each bracket shows the current total number of Players in the bracket as well as the number of courts needed for that bracket.
            
            ![Notice this bracket has 4 Players, which means it will need just one court. ](Round%20Robin%20Ladder%20App/image%202.png)
            
            Notice this bracket has 4 Players, which means it will need just one court. 
            
        3. Drag and Drop anywhere on the Player to move them to another bracket. You can also move them to the “Not Playing’ bracket if they are out for a Series.
            
            ![chrome_kFUEA6iEBJ.gif](Round%20Robin%20Ladder%20App/chrome_kFUEA6iEBJ.gif)
            
4. Choose Round Robin or Ladder, then the number of brackets or player per bracket, then fine-time the Players in each bracket in the Brackets area at the bottom of the screen. 
5. Click “Create Games” when you are the correct players in the correct bracket.

# Recording Scores

## Saving a New Game

1. Click “Enter Score” below the Team whose score you want to enter. 
2. Click the number corresponding the team’s score
3. If the other team has an empty score, the system will then prompt you to enter the score of the other team.
    
    ![chrome_FUt5CjWU7w.gif](Round%20Robin%20Ladder%20App/chrome_FUt5CjWU7w.gif)
    

## Clearing a Score

1. Click on the score you want to clear.
2. Click “Clear Score.” 

## Changing a Score

1. Click the score you want to change.
2. Click on the new score.

## Play Interface (Recording Scores Mode)

While simplicity remains the primary goal, the Play interface includes several features designed to enhance usability and efficiency. 

![Round Robin Play Interface](Round%20Robin%20Ladder%20App/Round_Robin_Play_Interface.png)

Round Robin Play Interface

### Menu

- Play - the current view, allows Players or Hosts to edit Games, change and view other Rounds, and enter scores
- Results - enter the Results view. This Menu option will appear after 1 Game has been scored.
- Settings- allows you to manage Series, download Games to DUPR, and Swap players.

### Your Game

This section, located at the top of the screen with a blue background, displays game information based on your role:

- **For Players**: If you are a logged-in Player participating in the Round Robin, this area will show the Game you are currently playing.
- **For Playing Hosts**: If you are a logged-in Host who is also playing in the Round Robin, this area will display the Game you are currently participating in.
- **For Non-Playing Hosts**: If you are a logged-in Host not playing in the Round Robin, this area will display games from the current round that still require scoring. After you enter the scores for a Game, any remaining unscored games from the round will appear here.

<aside>
💡

DailyPB determines the current Round by examining all Games across all rounds and identifying the first Game, in sequential order of Rounds, that does not have two scores recorded. The Round containing that Game is considered the current round.

</aside>

### **Forward**

Visible only on desktop, the forward area is clickable and allows you to advance to the next round. If the logged-in user is playing, it will also display your partner's name or indicate if you have a "bye.”

### **Back**

Visible only on desktop, the back area is clickable and allows you to move to the previous round. If the logged-in user is playing, it will also display your partner's name or indicate if you had a "bye.”

### Rounds

This area displays all rounds in the current Round Robin. The currently selected round is highlighted in bold. Completed rounds—where all games have two scores—are marked with a checkmark. When the page is loaded or refreshed, the current round will automatically be selected.

### All Games in Current Round

This section displays all games in the currently selected round, including the game shown in the "Your Game" area. Logged-in players can use this section to enter scores for other games or view their results.

# Viewing Round Robin Results

To view the results of a Round Robin (or Round Robin Series), simply click on the Results text at the top of page.

Results are displayed by bracket. They are sorted by:

1. Win - loss record
2. Average Differential - Total points for your team minus total points against your team divided by total games played.

If you see a record that is incorrect, click on the Play menu option, find the game in question, and change the score.

## Viewing Individual Player Results

Click on any Player to bring up their individual results. It will provide: their overall record, win percentage, average differential, and detailed results for each game, along with the corresponding bracket and time of recording.  

If multiple Series were recorded in the session, you can choose to view summarized results or navigate between results for each individual Series.

# Swapping Players During Play

Hosts can swap out any Player who has not finished any of their Games with another Attendee that is not currently playing in the same Round or another Bracket.  

![chrome_gL9F9fYMix.gif](Round%20Robin%20Ladder%20App/chrome_gL9F9fYMix.gif)

1. Click on the Swap button inside the Settings menu
2. Choose the Player you want to swap out
3. Choose the Round(s) in which you want to swap players
4. Choose the Attendee you want to swap in
5. Click the Confirm button

### Swapping FAQ

***Can I swap someone on the Waitlist?***

No, you will need to add them to the Attendees before they are available to swap.

***Can I swap Players for individual Rounds or only the entire Round Robin?***

Both! You have the opportunity to select which Rounds you would like to swap. You can click “Select All” to select all available Rounds.

***What if an attending player removes themselves from the Session after I have all the games created?*** 

The round robin function is designed to give you a warning anytime there are games with less than four players. If this happens a screen will pop up letting you know that one of the players has been removed from the Session after games were created and it will prompt you to delete all the games and then recreate them. 

***What if I screwed up and need to reverse a swap?***

No problem!  Just do the same process again and swap the original Player back in. As long as the original swapped out Player has not been added to another Bracket or swapped for another player in the same Round, they will be available to swap back.  

***What if I need to swap someone with a someone that is not yet signed up?***

That person needs to be signed up to the Session first in order to be available to swap. Make sure they are Attending and not Waitlisted when they sign up. 

***What if the Player I want to swap out is not showing up in the list of Players to swap?***

Most likely the Player you want to swap doesn’t have any incomplete Games. Go back to Play mode and look through their Games. (You can quickly find Rounds with incomplete Games by looking which Rounds don’t have a check-mark next to the Round number.) 

***When selecting the Rounds to swap, why are some Rounds not showing at all and others are greyed-out / not selectable?***

If a Round number doesn’t show up, that means the selected outgoing Player does not have a Game in that Round. If a Round shows up but is greyed out and not selectable, that means the Game is complete (both team scores entered) - complete Games are not eligible for a Player swap.

# FAQ

***What is the difference between a Round Robin and a Round Robin Ladder?***

The main difference is that a **Round Robin** involves players competing against everyone in their bracket in a single series, while a **Round Robin Ladder** completes a full series and then uses the results to adjust brackets, moving top players up and others down, before starting the next series.

***What are the maximum number of total Players and total brackets a Round Robin or Ladder can have?***

Each bracket can have a maximum of 24 Players. A Round Robin can have up to 4 brackets. This allows for a total of 96 players. There is also a minimum of 4 Players per bracket.

If you need to have more than 96 players or more than 4 brackets, simply create another Session.

What is a Round Robin “Series”?

A Series is a full Round Robin. If you are playing in a Round Robin Ladder, then after one Series is complete, a host can then move Players up and down brackets before starting a new Round Robin series. You can navigate Series and delete in the Series menu.

***Can I swap Players in the middle of a Round Robin?***

No, not at this time. To change Players, at least one game would need to be recorded to view the Results, and from there a new Series could be staged. Another option would be to delete the current Series, refresh the screen, and start over.

***Can anyone enter a score, or only a Host?***

Both the Host and anyone Attending the Session can add or edit scores. In practice, we’ve found that a few Players familiar with DailyPB often take the lead in entering scores for others.

Anyone with the link to the Round Robin can view the details but will not have permission to edit anything.
