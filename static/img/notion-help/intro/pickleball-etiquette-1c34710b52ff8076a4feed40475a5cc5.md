# Pickleball Etiquette

**Before the Game:**

- **Introduce yourself:** Be friendly and introduce yourself and your partner to your opponents before the match.
- **Learn names:** Try to learn the names of your opponents and partners, making the experience more social and welcoming.
- **Warm-up appropriately:** If you are warming up for your own match, do so only when allowed and in a way that doesn't disrupt other players or spectators.

**During the Game:**

- **Call out the score:** It's customary to call out the score (your side's score, then your opponents, then your serve position) before each serve.
- **Be honest and accurate:** Maintain honesty and integrity in scoring and line calls, giving your opponents the benefit of the doubt.
- **Respect the court:** Be mindful of time restrictions for court usage and finish your game within the allotted time if applicable.
- **Respect the lines:** Don't step into someone else's court while retrieving your ball; wait for a break in their game.
- **Yell "Ball" loudly:** If your ball goes into another court, yell "BALL" loudly to alert the players on that court.
- **Avoid unsolicited coaching:** Don't offer coaching advice unless asked for.
- **Support your partner:** Show respect for your partner's skills and avoid criticizing their actions negatively.
- **Stay positive:** Avoid outbursts, trash talk, or negativity.
- **Congratulate opponents:** Acknowledge great shots and good sportsmanship from your opponents.
- **Play with all skill levels:** Don't always target the weaker player for points or take advantage of someone's physical limitations.
