# Liability Waiver

DailyPB does not currently have an integrated method for players to sign a liability waiver (although expect this in 2025). However, here are some best practices regarding this subject. 

# Add some text to your Event description

This is the easiest method. Simply add text regarding liability limitations for you as a host, the venue, other participants, and related parties to the Event description.

You can generate this language by:

- consulting an attorney
- copying from another site (though you might still want to run it by your attorney)
- using an LLM to generate this (i.e., ChatGPT)

Here is an example generated by ChatGPT.

<aside>
📝

**Release of Liability Agreement**
By signing up for this pickleball event, I acknowledge and agree to assume all risks associated with participation, including but not limited to injury, disability, death, or property damage. I release and hold harmless the event organizers, sponsors, hosts, volunteers, officials, venue owners, and any associated parties from any claims or liabilities arising out of my participation, including those caused by negligence. I certify that I am physically fit, will follow all event rules, and consent to medical treatment if needed. I also grant permission for the use of any photos or videos of me taken during the event for promotional purposes. By registering, I confirm that I have read, understand, and agree to these terms.

</aside>

# Use DailyPB Email Templates + Signature Service

This method is straightforward but requires a monthly subscription for the digital signature service.

1. First, sign up for a digital signature service that handles legal documents. We've personally used [Waiverfile.com](http://Waiverfile.com) and recommend it. They offer legal document templates designed specifically for sports waivers.
2. Next, create a DailyPB Signup Template through the [Host](http://dailypb.com/host) page by clicking on Templates. This template will generate the email sent to players when they sign up for your session. Customize the template, but be sure to include instructions for accessing your signature service link.
    
    ![image.png](Liability%20Waiver/image.png)
    
3. When editing your Event, navigate to the last (fifth) page and link the Template you just created for Player signups. 
    
    ![image.png](Liability%20Waiver/7c62b93c-36fa-4505-b020-b9f4b08a70f0.png)
    
4. Before your Session begins, cross-reference all Session Signups with the waiver signatures in your signature service. Contact anyone missing a signature ahead of time, or have them complete it before participating.
